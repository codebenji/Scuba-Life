Scuba Diving in the Asia

Indonesia

Bali: Bali offers a variety of dive sites catering to different skill levels. The USS Liberty Shipwreck in Tulamben and the vibrant reefs around Nusa Penida are popular spots. Komodo: Komodo National Park is known for its strong currents and diverse marine life. It's a great place to see manta rays, sharks, and other pelagic species. Raja Ampat: Raja Ampat is considered a biodiversity hotspot with some of the most diverse marine life in the world. It's a paradise for underwater photographers and experienced divers.

Maldives

The Maldives is made up of over 1,000 coral islands, offering a wide range of dive sites. From shallow lagoons to deep channels, you can encounter manta rays, whale sharks, colorful coral reefs, and a variety of fish species.

Thailand

Similan Islands: The Similans are part of a national park and offer some of the best diving in Thailand. Rich marine life, clear waters, and opportunities to see large pelagic creatures are highlights. Koh Tao: Koh Tao is known for its affordable dive training and beautiful underwater scenery. It's a popular destination for new divers, offering a mix of shallow sites and deeper dive sites.

Philippines:

Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is renowned for its pristine coral reefs and diverse marine life. It's a remote destination and is only accessible by liveaboard trips during certain times of the year. Apo Reef: Apo Reef is the largest contiguous coral reef system in the Philippines. It's a great place for wall diving, spotting sharks, and enjoying colorful coral gardens.

Malaysia:

Sipadan Island: Sipadan is famous for its stunning wall diving and diverse marine ecosystem. The island is known for its steep drop-offs and the possibility of encountering large schools of barracuda, turtles, and sharks. Mabul Island: Mabul is known for its macro diving opportunities, making it a paradise for underwater photographers. The nearby Sipadan Island adds to the overall diving experience.
